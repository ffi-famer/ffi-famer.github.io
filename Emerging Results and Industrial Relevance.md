## Emerging Results and Industrial Relevance

The completed FAMER papers show a clear progression of results. First, they identify how requirements are represented and shared across multiple stakeholders in machine learning-based automotive perception development. Second, they establish data annotation as a key requirements engineering concern, showing that annotation decisions directly influence system quality, safety, and validation. Third, they reveal practical challenges in managing annotation requirements and recurring annotation quality problems in industrial autonomous-driving contexts. Finally, they propose structured solutions such as DARS to represent and specify annotation requirements in a more traceable and reusable way.

For the industrial partners, these results provide practical value. Volvo Cars and Zenseact benefit from stronger requirements alignment, traceability, and multi-party collaboration support. Kognic benefits from clearer foundations for annotation requirements, ground-truth production, and annotation-tool improvement. RISE benefits through stronger support for safety assurance, industrial best practices, and innovation services. Together, these results show that FAMER is building both scientific knowledge and practical methods for safe AI-based perception-system development.
